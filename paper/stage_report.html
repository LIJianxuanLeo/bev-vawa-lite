Stage Report:
A Lightweight Vision-Action / World-Action Navigation Stack
with Reactive Safety Augmentation on the PIB-Nav Benchmark

BEV-VAWA-Lite Project  ·  April 2026
We report interim results on BEV-VAWA-Lite, a lightweight depth-only PointGoal navigation stack combining a reactive Vision-Action (VA) branch with a short-horizon latent World-Action (WA) branch over a shared bird's-eye-view (BEV) state. The system is trained entirely on a consumer-grade Apple M4 laptop using a procedural MuJoCo benchmark (PIB-Nav). This report consolidates four milestones: (i) a full-scale retrain that raised Success Rate (SR) at our headline seed from 0.39 to 0.45 over 100 held-out episodes; (ii) two on-policy ablations isolating the contribution of the WA branch and its rollout horizon; (iii) a metric hygiene fix for pathological SPL values; and (iv) a reactive safety override — an artificial-potential-field (APF) correction on the forward arc augmented with an independent lateral-sector scrape guard — that improves SR by +0.093 ± 0.013 paired across four seeds (+8 to +11 pp, never negative), bringing mean SR to 0.49 and matching the reactive-safety A⋆ baseline (0.50). We include a per-seed analysis showing that while the absolute per-seed SR has a ±5 pp spread on this small test set, the safety improvement is approximately 4× more consistent than the raw per-seed variance. We close with a roadmap toward latent-dynamics and reachability supervision on the Gibson PointNav v2 track, motivated by the residual failure modes that reactive augmentation provably cannot address.

1  Background and Scope

BEV-VAWA-Lite targets the static, depth-only, indoor PointGoal slice of embodied navigation [1], where recent foundation-scale systems [12, 11, 13] are arguably overbuilt. The stack factorises the problem into a fast reactive branch that proposes K=5 candidate waypoints on a fan anchor, and a slow evaluator that rolls out each candidate in a latent BEV for a short horizon H=3, reading out collision risk, goal progress, and epistemic uncertainty from a three-member ensemble. Candidates are re-ranked by an analytic fusion rule

Qk = α · s̃k + β · p̂k − γ · σ(r̂k) − δ · ûk,    (1)

where s̃, p̂, r̂, û are the soft-maxed VA score, predicted progress, risk logit, and uncertainty, and (α, β, γ, δ) = (1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 0.5). The winning anchor is converted to (v, ω) commands by a pure-pursuit controller [4].

The four milestones reported here concern the PIB-Nav track (MuJoCo, laptop-local). The parallel Gibson [11] remote track described in Section 7 is in planning and is explicitly out of scope for the numbers below.

2  Milestone 1: Full-Scale PIB-Nav Retraining

Protocol. We regenerated the offline expert dataset with 1000 procedural rooms and 64 samples per room (cf. the initial 200×32 regime), and ran the three-stage curriculum with (eA, eB, eC) = (20, 20, 8) epochs. Stage A supervises the encoder and VA head with cross-entropy on the expert-matched anchor plus a Huber term on the selected offset; Stage B freezes the encoder and fits the WA heads with binary cross-entropy (risk), mean squared error (progress), and ensemble variance (uncertainty); Stage C unfreezes the final encoder block and the two heads jointly.

Result. On a held-out 100-episode suite (Table 1, row BEV-VAWA (full)), SR rose from 0.39 (prior 200×32, epochs 6/6/3 baseline) to 0.45, with CollisionRate dropping from 0.65 to 0.58. SPL improved commensurately to 0.44. All subsequent results use the retrained checkpoint runs/default/stage_c.pt. Total wall-clock on the M4: ~5.5h data generation + ~3h training, fully on-device.

3  Milestone 2: On-Policy Ablations

Two targeted ablations isolate the two non-obvious design choices of the WA branch: (a) its existence, and (b) its rollout horizon.

(a) no_wa. At inference time, the WA branch is silenced by setting (γ, δ) = (0, 0) in Eq. 1 so that only the VA score and an analytic progress proxy drive candidate selection. No retraining is required — the ablation reuses the full-model checkpoint. SR drops to 0.39 (−6 pp) and CollisionRate rises to 0.63. This quantifies the WA branch's end-task contribution under identical encoder parameters.

(b) h1. The rollout horizon of the WA branch is shortened from H=3 to H=1 and stages B and C are retrained from the shared Stage-A checkpoint. SR ends at 0.44 (−1 pp). The near-null delta is informative: with only risk / progress / uncertainty end-state supervision and no intermediate-step objective, the longer rollout adds few gradients and thus little expressive power. This observation motivates the latent-dynamics loss Ldyn in Section 7, which supervises the per-step rollout latents directly, as proposed for latent world models in [5, 6].

4  Milestone 3: SPL Metric Hygiene

A latent bug in the evaluation harness propagated NaN values into SPL and PathLenRatio whenever the closed-loop evaluator's grid-based reference planner failed to connect start and goal, which the code silently replaced by ∞. For unsuccessful episodes in that regime the SPL = ℓ⋆ / ℓagent definition [1] degenerates to ∞/∞ = NaN, contaminating both per-method summaries and the cross-method aggregate. We patched bev_vawa/eval/metrics.py to conservatively emit SPL = 0 for failed episodes with non-finite reference path length, and to exclude such episodes from the PLR mean. All four milestones were re-evaluated under the corrected metric; the SPL column of Table 1 reflects the post-fix values.

5  Milestone 4: A Reactive Safety Layer

Inspection of the 55 failures at the full checkpoint revealed that ~95% were collision-budget exhaustions (10 successive contacts), not time-outs or goal-divergence. We therefore developed a reactive safety override: a depth-only module that observes ot and perturbs the policy command (v, ω) in-place, with zero training and zero access to internal model state. The module is a post-hoc shim; the learned policy is unchanged.

5.1  Design

The override combines an artificial-potential-field (APF) forward corrector in the spirit of Khatib [7] with an independent lateral-sector scrape guard. Depth pixels outside a small valid threshold are discarded, and all clearance statistics use the 5th percentile of remaining pixels, which is robust to MuJoCo depth misses.

Forward APF. Let dmin denote the minimum clearance in a 60%-wide forward column band (±27° at FOV 90°). When dmin < dwarn the wrapper adds an additive angular correction scaled by left/right depth asymmetry within the arc and by a linear urgency ramp κ ∈ [0, 1]:

ωfwd = asym · ωmax · (0.5 + 0.5 κ),    (2)

with [dnear, dwarn] = [0.35, 0.60] m. Linear speed is never zeroed — in the innermost band the controller still emits v = 0.3 vmax while rotating. An asymmetry tiebreaker (falling back to the policy's own ω sign, then a fixed left bias) prevents spin-in-place lock, which we verified as a dominant failure mode of hard-gated reactive layers [3, 10].

Lateral scrape guard. The forward arc alone is blind to side-scrapes — episodes in which depth directly ahead is clear but the robot's shoulder grazes an obstacle during a turn. We add two independent lateral sectors covering the outer 20% of image columns on each side (approximately ±27° to ±45°), computing side-specific closeness scores cℓ, cr ∈ [0, 1] from the same clearance statistic. The side correction and a mild speed taper are always evaluated, summed with Eq. 2, and clipped:

ωside = (cr − cℓ) · g · ωmax,    vmult = 1 − (1 − τ) · max(cℓ, cr),
ω′ = clip(ω + ωfwd + ωside, −ωmax, +ωmax),   v′ = vnew · vmult,

with (g, τ, dside) = (0.6, 0.85, 0.40 m).

5.2  Results

Headline-seed result. At seed 12345 the safety override raises SR to 0.53, SPL to 0.52, and lowers CollisionRate to 0.50 (Table 1). Applied on top of the privileged A⋆ planner the same override reduces its collision rate from 0.54 to 0.50, confirming that the augmentation is policy-agnostic.

Paired cross-seed analysis. To separate the safety improvement from per-seed noise we re-ran both full and full+Safety at three additional seeds {42, 7, 31337}. The mean over four seeds is 0.40 ± 0.05 → 0.49 ± 0.05 on SR and 0.39 ± 0.05 → 0.49 ± 0.05 on SPL. The per-seed paired deltas are {+0.08, +0.09, +0.09, +0.11} on SR, giving ΔSR = +0.093 ± 0.013 and an analogous ΔColl = −0.072. In other words, although absolute per-seed SR has a σ of 0.05 on this small test set, the paired safety improvement has a σ of only 0.013 — a 4× reduction in variance that makes the improvement statistically clean even in a low-n regime. We regard this as the primary claim of Milestone 4; the headline-seed +8 pp number is an illustrative single case inside this distribution.

Relation to the A⋆ upper bound. The A⋆ baseline in Table 1 is a controller-limited oracle: it replans on the ground-truth inflated occupancy grid every step, but tracks the resulting path with the same pure-pursuit controller as the learned policy. At seed 12345 the learned policy plus safety exceeds this upper bound by +5 pp SR; on the 4-seed mean it sits within 1 pp of both the A⋆ upper bound (0.48) and the A⋆+Safety row (0.50), slightly below the latter. Rather than claiming the learned policy surpasses the geometric planner as a general statement, the honest reading is: the learned+safety configuration is statistically indistinguishable from A⋆+safety on this benchmark, while being a pure neural-network policy with no access to the ground-truth occupancy grid at inference time. The stronger claim — that test-time augmentation can close the gap to a controller-limited oracle — is what survives the seed sweep.

6  Discussion

1. Test-time augmentation can close the gap to a controller-limited planner upper bound. Practitioners often treat A⋆ as an absolute ceiling; our results show this is misleading when the A⋆ trajectory feeds a non-ideal tracker. Averaged over four seeds, reactive safety augmentation brings the learned policy to within 1 pp of both the bare A⋆ baseline and the A⋆+Safety baseline (Table 1). At one seed it even overshoots by +5 pp, but the cross-seed mean is the claim we endorse.

2. Reactive augmentation has a structural ceiling. The 47% residual failure rate of the final system is dominated by cases that no forward-facing depth-only reactive module can resolve: dead-end corridors, tight U-shaped alcoves, and crowded-corner spawns. These require either global re-planning or structural changes to the learned policy itself. The WA branch's h1 ablation already hinted at the direction: supervision on intermediate rollout latents, and a reachability-aware fusion term, as specified next.

7  Seed-Stability Protocol

PIB-Nav is a deliberately small benchmark — 100 procedural test rooms at a fixed seed — and we observe that the absolute per-seed SR of the learned policy has a standard deviation of ≈0.05 on this test set (Table 2). The practical implications we adopt throughout this report are:

  1. Headline numbers are mean ± std over 4 seeds {12345, 42, 7, 31337} for the two learned-policy rows in Table 1.
  2. Ablations are interpreted as paired deltas at the same seed, not as absolute SR. Per-seed offset cancels in the ΔSR column. The single-seed SR level of a given ablation is therefore treated as a diagnostic, not as a claim.
  3. The no_unc ablation is byte-identical to full at seed 12345: setting δ=0 in the fusion equation changes no candidate ranking. Combined with per-seed variance this is evidence that the uncertainty term δû as currently implemented does not meaningfully influence test-time decisions, and will be either re-calibrated or retired in the Gibson-v2 re-design.
  4. Paired improvements with σ well below the raw per-seed σ are the strongest evidence. The safety override qualifies (σΔ=0.013 vs. raw σ=0.05).

8  Forward Plan

Two upgrades are planned for the remote Gibson PointNav v2 track [11], each addressing a specific failure mode identified above.

BEV Layer 2: geometrically-grounded multi-channel BEV. The current encoder applies adaptive pooling to a CNN feature map; it does not perform an explicit metric back-projection of the depth frame onto the floor plane. Gibson-v2 introduces a differentiable geometry lift producing a three-channel BEV (occ, free, goal-prior) of resolution 64 × 64, bringing the representation closer to the canonical BEV designs in autonomous driving [9, 8].

WA Layer 2: latent dynamics Ldyn and dead-end head. Inspired by latent world models [5, 6] and reachability prediction in safe RL [2], the WA rollout latents ẑt+τ will be supervised against a no-grad future encoder pass over τ ∈ {1, …, H} future frames, and a per-candidate dead-end logit dk will be trained via binary cross-entropy using Habitat's pathfinder.find_path as the label oracle. The fusion rule is extended to

Qkv2 = Qk − η · σ(dk),  η = 1.0,

directly addressing the residual failure cluster isolated in Section 6: dead-end avoidance is reachability rather than clearance, and is invisible to any forward-looking reactive module.

9  Conclusion

Within the depth-only, laptop-trained PIB-Nav regime we demonstrate: a data-and-epoch-scaling improvement at the headline seed (Milestone 1); a clean decomposition of the WA branch's end-task contribution with a null finding on the uncertainty term (Milestone 2); a metric-side correctness fix (Milestone 3); and a reactive safety override that delivers a paired SR improvement of +0.093 ± 0.013 across four seeds, closing the gap to the controller-limited A⋆ upper bound in the cross-seed mean and exceeding it at the headline seed (Milestone 4). The remaining ≈50% failure mass is architecturally structural: it requires latent-dynamics and reachability supervision on a geometrically grounded BEV, which is the subject of the ongoing Gibson PointNav v2 track.
